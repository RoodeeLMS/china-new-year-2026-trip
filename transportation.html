Getting Around in China

Plan your transportation for flights, local travel, and inter-city connections.

Airport Transportation:

  • Bangkok (Suvarnabhumi) - Departure: Hotel to airport transfer
  • Hangzhou Xiaoshan Airport - Arrival: Airport to hotel transfer
  • Return Flight: Hotel to Hangzhou airport
  • Options: Shuttle bus, taxi, ride-sharing (Didi), private driver

Hangzhou Local Transport:

  • Metro/Subway: Modern, clean, efficient (metro card available)
  • Bus: Extensive network, very cheap
  • Taxi: Available and affordable, use meter or apps
  • Didi Chuxing: China's ride-sharing app (similar to Uber)
  • Bike Share: Mobike or Ofo for short distances
  • Walking: West Lake area is very walkable

Transportation Cards & Apps:

  • Metro Card: Buy at airport or stations (rechargeable)
  • Didi App: Download before arrival for ride-sharing
  • Maps Apps: Baidu Maps or Amap (better than Google Maps in China)
  • Payment Apps: WeChat Pay or Alipay for fares

Inter-City Travel (if exploring beyond Hangzhou):

  • High-Speed Rail: Fast, clean, comfortable (G-trains or D-trains)
  • Regular Trains: Longer, cheaper option
  • Flights: For longer distances (Shanghai, Beijing)
  • Long-distance Buses: Available but less comfortable

Guangzhou Transfer (Overnight Jan 2-3):

  • Airport hotel or lounge for overnight stay
  • Airport metro line connects airport to city
  • Plan meal and rest options during transfer
  • Luggage storage services available if needed

Hangzhou to Guangzhou Route:

  • Distance: ~800 km, multiple options
  • High-speed train: ~2.5-3 hours (Shanghai to Guangzhou)
  • Regular train: ~8-10 hours
  • Domestic flights: ~2.5 hours flight time
  • Coach/bus: ~12-14 hours (economy option)

Transportation Costs (Estimates):

  • Airport transfer (both directions): $20-50
  • Metro card: 50-100 CNY ($7-14)
  • Daily taxi/ride-share: $5-15
  • Buses: 0.5-3 CNY per ride ($0.07-0.40)

Tips:

  • Have phone charged for navigation apps
  • Keep small bills/coins for buses
  • Download offline maps before arrival
  • Peak hours: 7-9 AM, 5-7 PM on weekdays
  • Book high-speed rail tickets in advance (especially around Chinese New Year)
